Summary

Iñaki Garitano is a researcher and lecturer in the Electronics and Computer Science Department and the Data Analysis and Cybersecurity group at Mondragon Unibertsitatea. He holds a PhD titled Behavioral Modeling for Anomaly Detection in Industrial Control Systems and an MSc in Telecommunication Engineering, both from Mondragon Unibertsitatea. Before joining Mondragon Unibertsitatea in 2015, he worked at Universitetssenteret på Kjeller (UNIK) as a postdoctoral researcher, focusing on measurable Security, Privacy, and Dependability (SPD), Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), and Internet of Things (IoT). In 2018 and 2019, he received a grant from the National Cybersecurity Institute (INCIBE) for a second postdoc, during which he focused on various research areas within the current research group. Additionally, he serves as the Chief Technology Officer of the Basic Internet Foundation. Currently, he is participating in several European and National public funding research projects and has authored several journal, conference, and book chapters.

Specialties: Anomaly Detection Systems, Intrusion Detection Systems, ICS Security, Computer and Network Security, Data Analysis.

Interests: Secure Computing.
